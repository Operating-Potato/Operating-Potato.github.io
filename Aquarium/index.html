This is an animated virtual gallery of various fish species.

A fish is an animal that generally lives in water, breathes primarily with gills, has fins, and is ectothermic, though there are exceptions to all of these.

    The three primary groups of fish still extant today are:

  • Jawless fish: fish that don't have a jaw to open and close. Currently represented by lampreys and hagfish.
  • Cartilaginous fish: sharks, rays, chimaeras, etc. Have a skeleton made of cartilage rather than bone.
  • Bony fish: the vast majority of fish on earth now. Have a skeleton made of bone. Divided into two groups. Lobe-finned fish have a lobe surrounded by fleshy parts, and are the ancestors of all land vertebrates, and include coelacanths and lungfish. Ray-finned fish have spiny fins with membranes between the spines, and are probably what you think of when you think "fish."
